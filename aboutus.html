About Us

Welcome to our website dedicated to the forts of Maharashtra. Here, you can explore the rich history and architecture of these magnificent structures.

Our mission is to provide detailed information about each fort, including their historical significance, architectural features, and visitor information.

We hope you find our content informative and inspiring as you learn more about the forts that played a crucial role in Maharashtra's history.

Raigad

Raigad Fort, located in Maharashtra’s Sahyadri range, was chosen by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj as the capital of the Maratha Empire in 1674. It stands 2,700 feet above sea level and symbolizes Maratha strength, independence, and architectural brilliance. Today, it remains a historic landmark and key tourist attraction.

Rajgad

Rajgad, meaning “King of Forts,” is a historic hill fort in Pune district, Maharashtra. Formerly called Murumbdev, it was the first capital of the Maratha Empire under Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj for 26 years. The fort stands about 1,376 meters above sea level and played a crucial role in Maratha history.

Torna

Torna Fort, also known as Prachandagad, is a historic fort in Maharashtra, India. It was the first fort captured by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj at the age of 16 in 1643. Located at an elevation of 1,403 meters, it offers stunning views of the surrounding Sahyadri mountains and is a popular trekking destination.
